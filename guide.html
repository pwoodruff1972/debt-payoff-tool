Debt Snowball vs. Debt Avalanche

A complete guide to choosing the right debt payoff strategy for you.

Choosing a strategy is the most powerful step you can take to get out of debt. Instead of just making minimum payments and hoping for the best, a strategy gives you a clear plan and a target to focus on. The two most popular and effective methods are the Debt Snowball and the Debt Avalanche. Let's break them down.

The Debt Snowball Method

The Debt Snowball method focuses on momentum and motivation. The idea is to pay off your smallest debts first, regardless of their interest rates, to score quick wins and build confidence.

How It Works

  1. List all of your debts from the smallest balance to the largest.
  2. Make the minimum payment on every debt.
  3. Put every extra dollar you have towards the smallest debt until it's paid off.
  4. Once the smallest debt is gone, take all the money you were paying on it (its minimum payment plus your extra payment) and roll it into the payment for the *next* smallest debt.
  5. Repeat this process. As you pay off each debt, your "snowball" of payment money grows larger, knocking out the bigger debts faster and faster.

Pros

  • Highly Motivating: Scoring quick wins by closing accounts provides powerful psychological reinforcement to keep going.
  • Builds Habits: The feeling of progress helps build and maintain the habit of making extra payments.

Cons

  • Costs More: Because you aren't prioritizing high-interest debts, you will likely pay more in total interest over the life of your loans.

The Debt Avalanche Method

The Debt Avalanche method is the purely mathematical approach. It focuses on paying off your debts with the highest interest rates (APR) first, which saves you the most money over time.

How It Works

  1. List all of your debts from the highest APR to the lowest.
  2. Make the minimum payment on every debt.
  3. Put every extra dollar you have towards the debt with the highest interest rate.
  4. Once that debt is gone, roll all the money you were paying on it into the payment for the debt with the *next* highest APR.
  5. Repeat this process until you are debt-free.

Pros

  • Saves the Most Money: This is the mathematically optimal strategy. It guarantees you will pay the least amount of interest possible.
  • Fastest Overall Payoff: By reducing the amount of interest you pay, you can become debt-free slightly faster than with the Snowball method.

Cons

  • Can Feel Slower: If your highest-interest debt also has a very large balance, it can take a long time to pay it off, which can be discouraging.

Which Method is Right for You?

The best method is the one you will actually stick with.

  • If you need **motivation and quick wins** to stay on track, the **Debt Snowball** is probably the best choice for you.
  • If you are highly disciplined and want to **save the most money possible**, the **Debt Avalanche** is the superior financial choice.
