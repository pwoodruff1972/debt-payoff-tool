Why Spreadsheets are a Great Starting Point

For decades, spreadsheets have been the go-to tool for personal finance management. They're powerful, flexible, and give you a hands-on feel for your numbers. Creating a debt snowball plan in a tool like Excel or Google Sheets is a fantastic first step towards taking control of your finances.

You can list your debts, set your budget, and manually calculate your progress. But as many people discover, the manual approach comes with a few common frustrations.

The Hidden Problems with Using a Spreadsheet

  • They are entirely manual. You have to remember to update every single balance, every single month. Forgetting one step can throw off the whole plan.
  • Formulas can break easily. Accidentally deleting a single formula can break the entire calculation, and it can be difficult to find the error.
  • Comparing strategies is difficult. Want to see if the Debt Avalanche would save you more money? You'd have to completely rebuild and re-sort your spreadsheet.
  • They are not mobile-friendly. Trying to view and update a complex spreadsheet on your phone is often a frustrating experience.
