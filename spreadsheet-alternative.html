The Appeal of the Debt Spreadsheet

For decades, spreadsheet software like Microsoft Excel and Google Sheets has been the go-to for people looking to take control of their finances. It makes sense: they are flexible, powerful, and many people already have them.

However, when it comes to creating and managing a long-term debt payoff plan, the manual nature of a spreadsheet introduces several hidden risks and frustrations.

The Downsides of a Manual Spreadsheet

  • Easy to Break: One wrong formula or an accidental deletion can break the entire calculation, leading to an incorrect payoff plan without you even realizing it.
  • Time-Consuming: You have to manually update every single balance, every single month. To change strategies from Snowball to Avalanche, you have to re-sort and re-calculate everything by hand.
  • Not Truly Mobile-Friendly: While you can open a spreadsheet on your phone, they are often difficult to use and view on a small screen.
  • Not Motivating: A wall of numbers in a grid isn't very inspiring. It's hard to visualize your progress and celebrate your wins.
